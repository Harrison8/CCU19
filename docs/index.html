Exploring Venture Capital in Louisville

These graphs explore Venture Capital investment in Louisville between 2008 and 2019 based on data from Pitchbook. I included the ability to toggle between different years of data and ways of adjusting the data if you want to explore. The defaults I selected are the ones I think tell the story best, so you can scroll through and get all the whole picture without toggling. My main takeaways are in bold.



Raw data

These graphs show the raw amount of venture capital investment, without any other adjustments.

Note: Since we’re only partway through 2019, investment in our peer cities appears to have dropped off. Normally I would remove 2019, but since it is an important year for an accurate picture of Louisville’s VC, I left it in. Also sorry some text runs off the graphs; I’ll fix it before we distribute these.

Louisville has generally seen low levels of VC investment compared to peer cities, though there was much more VC investment activity in 2018 and 2019. The data isn’t super comparable without adjusting for population, though.



Total Venture Capital

These graphs are adjusted for the population size of each city.

  • The Unadjusted graphs show the raw values.
  • The Per capita graphs show VC investment per person.
  • The Per Lou graphs keep Louisville’s VC dollars at the same level and adjust other cities accordingly. For example, a city with twice the population as Louisville would have its VC dollars halved in the “scaled to Lou” graphs. The comparision is the exact same as the per capita graphs except for the dollar amounts. This might seem more natural to some people, especially those with an understanding of dollar amounts in the VC space. Just switch to “per capita” if this seems comfusing.)

Note: City size and VC Investment

Money follows money: larger cities attract more venture capital, even after adjusting for population. You would expect a city twice the size of Louisville to attract more than twice the amount of venture capital. These graphs compare average scaled VC investment with population.

The data is adjusted for population, so if all cities had the same investment per capita, they would be on a horizontal line. The blue line is a regression line showing the actual relationship based on the data.

Louisville performs about average from 2008 to 2019 and better than we would expect from 2017-2019. With the exception of Memphis, between 2008 and 2019, every city smaller than Louisville had lower VC investment per capita, and every city larger than Louisville had higher VC investment per capita. We are right where we would expect to be. We’re outperforming our peers when you look at 2017-2019.
